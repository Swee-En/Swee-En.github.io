Aloha

A 24-hour application design project

Project Overview

This project is part of SUTD's student-run hackathon, What The Hack 2021. Four themes were given to choose from. Then, we were to identify some problems related to the theme, and design an application to solve that problem within 24 hours.

Task

Design an application

Time

24 hours

Skills

Prototyping

Tools

Figma

Chosen Theme

The theme that our team had chosen was Transportation.

We brainstormed across several forms of transportations including bicycles, trains, cars and planes. Starting with problems relating to personal convenience, we moved to a larger scale such as considering the carbon footprint produced by the various vehicles, which contribute to global warming.

Identified Problem

After some thinking, our team decided to target the problem of the high carbon footprint produced, specifically during travels.

Car rentals is a very popular option when travelling, due to the convenience provided as opposed to taking public transport. However, cars also produce one of the largest amount of greenhouse gases. In addition, it is common for tourists to travel in small numbers, such as in pairs or even solo. In such cases, the space of the car is not fully utilised despite carrying the same amount of carbon footprint as it would when driving say, 5 pax.

Chosen Solution

As such, our team decided to design a travel carpooling application, where users can rent a car with other users whose destinations and schedules are in line with their own.

For the users, this has two advantages.

Saving on car rental fees

Finding travel companions

By co-renting a car, users are now able to save part of the initial rental fees. They will also have a chance to make friends overseas, which is a bonus especially for users who love to meet new people!

Prototype

Since this is a 24-hour design competition, we only conducted a general market and user research before jumping into creating the prototype. Its main features include allowing users to find carpooling buddies through an iternary search, chats, and an easy payment system.

Upon setting up an account and payment details, users will arrive at this homepage. Here, they can browse other users' iternary, which includes details such as travel destination, duration, and the number of carpooling buddies desired.

Alternatively, users can also create their own iternary and upload!

Users can chat with one another to discuss details before deciding to become carpooling buddies.

When both users have made the deal, a deposit will be made.

Half of the deposit will be returned at the start of the trip, and the remaining half at the end. This is to ensure that users make responsible decisions when confirming a booking.

Reflection

Since this was given a tight timeline of only 24 hours, not only were we unable to do an in-depth user research and competitor analysis, we were also unable to conduct any user testing for the refinement of our prototype. We also had no prior knowledge of UI design, hence the ideas were largely based on our personal knowledge and experience.

However, this was still my very first encounter with UI design, albeit a very brief one. Although it was rushed, we were still able to go through most of the typical process of brainstorming, researching, ideating, and prototyping. It served as a start to my interest in UI design, which spurred me to take the UI/UX module in the later years!
