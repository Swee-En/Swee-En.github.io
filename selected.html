Selected Works

Visual Novel

Visual novels have always been one of the things I wanted to try, and this year I finally attempted it. As a Genshin Impact and Childe fan, I decided to create a spin-off of the game and character, where the player goes on a Valentine's date with the character at Disneyland.

One of the beginning scenes with Childe on the train to Disneyland

A scene at the souvenir shop at Disneyland

One of the possible endings of this visual novel

While this is still a work-in-progress, I have created some assets, including the sprites, background images and special scenes. The illustrations were mainly done on iPad and ibispaint, whereas the programming was done on Ren'Py, a visual novel engine. This software was also originally used to create the very popular visual novel, Doki Doki Literature Club (DDLC), before the game was recreated in the Unity engine.

Requests

I have also received a request to draw an imaginary scene, where the story deviates from the original Yuri's route. This scene takes place where Yuri, reaching the height of her delirium, chases the main character in an attempt to take him down together with her.

While this was a difficult piece due to severals factors such as the lighting, it was extremely fun imagining an alternative scenario and translating others' ideas into drawing.

DTIYS

DTIYS, or Draw This In Your Style, is a self-organised competition where participants redraw the organiser's artwork. Participants can redraw the character in a different pose, setting, outfit, etc, allowing them to freely interpret the character in their own way. My favorite illustration is from a chess-themed DTIYS organised by @yutong901324.

Character Design

After drawing existing characters for a while, I had the desire to create my own. In the earlier years of my drawing journey, I had designed several original characters before, but never did go into any details. This was the first time I created design sheets for an original character, accounting for the various views, his outfits and even accesories.

I gave him the name Gabriel Corvy, with the inspiration for his design and personality derived from a bird called the Blue Jay.

Manga

Besides drawing illustrations, I have also attempted drawing manga. Initially, I started with trying to recreate pages of existing manga, then eventually made my own. These included 4-koma manga (4 panels in one page), entries in the Silent Manga Audition, as well as fan fiction manga based on existing stories.

A story about the reunion of Kanroji & Iguro, characters from Kimetsu no Yaiba, in their next lives because their deaths in the story made me too upset. The full manga can be viewed here.
